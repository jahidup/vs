Building Real Solutions for Salemgarh

Our robotics center goes beyond theoretical physics formulas. We teach students how to write physical C code to read digital pins and drive actuators. Our students develop solutions designed to address challenges in agriculture, electricity conservation, and automated recycling in Tamkuhi region.

By wiring microcontrollers, configuring solar tracking arrays, and interfacing wireless ESP8266 nodes, students learn electronics, physics, mathematics, and code optimization simultaneously. This practical understanding solidifies their secondary school physics syllabus while providing real computational skills.

Our Tech Showcase

Featured Student Hardware Projects

Smart Automated Irrigation

Built using soil moisture sensors, liquid solenoid valves, and ESP8266 microcontrollers. It monitors crop dryness levels and waters local plants automatically, sending logging data to a student web portal.

AI Trash sorter

Interfaced a USB camera module with a Raspberry Pi running basic classification code. It recognizes plastic, metal, and compostable trash, triggering servo gates to sort waste.

Gesture Controlled Drone

Quadcopter model assembled by secondary students utilizing MPU6050 gyroscope sensors and RF transmitter nodes. It translates hand movements into steady flight telemetry commands.

Solar Tracker Array

Uses Light Dependent Resistors (LDR) to track light angles, rotating small solar panels dynamically to maximize efficiency. Teaches physics calculations for light angles and panel dynamics.

Rural Innovation Workshops & Hackathons

Every quarter, Sankalp Digital Pathshala organizes the Kushinagar Rural Hackathon. This 24-hour event brings young innovators together to build functional technical solutions under mentorship. The workshops teach circuit debugging, Python syntax optimization, and user interface design. Top performing teams receive scholarships, certifications, and project display opportunities in our showcase hall.
